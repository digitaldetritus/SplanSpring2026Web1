Laura Splan is a New York City based artist working at the intersections of Science, Technology, and Culture. Her research-based studio practice and interdisciplinary collaborations culminate in multimedia exhibitions and events. Through a process of co-creation and ideation, she has worked with physicists, biologists, virologists, epigeneticists, mycologists, and engineers. Recent projects have included immersive multisensory experiences, interactive installations, networked devices, participatory sculptures, and intimately scaled objects. With an approach grounded in what she calls the “tactical tactile”, her work cultivates intuitive comprehension of the interconnectedness of scientific phenomena and the everyday. Often combining analog and digital processes, her artworks have reimagined viruses as lace, biometric data as sculpture, biophysical simulations as weavings, and 3D molecular models as environments navigated with bodily movement. By repurposing artifacts of the laboratory, Splan reveals the “GUI/gooey” or liminal spaces mediating our relationship to nature and to our bodies. Lab machines agitated by politically contested language and viral proteins animated with public health data poetically connect social and biological systems. These uncanny combinations offer new possibilities for understanding complexity through curiosity, wonder, and play with tangible encounters with seemingly intangible worlds.
